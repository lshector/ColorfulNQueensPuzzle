Colorful N Queens Puzzle Playground

The Colorful N-Queens puzzle (created by LinkedIn as the "Queens" game)
is a variation of the classical N-Queens problem.

The puzzle involves placing `N` colored queens on an `N x N` chessboard such that:
- Exactly one queen is placed in each row, column, and color region.
- No two queens are adjacent to each other (including diagonally)

In this website, you can play the puzzle and explore different algorithms
related to solving and/or generating the puzzle.

Play Mode



In play mode, you can click on the cells to try to solve the puzzle.

You may mark a cell with a '♛' to place a queen or with an 'x' to indicate that
no queen should be placed there.

If a queen is placed in an invalid position, the conflicting cells will be highlighted.

Solve Mode

In solve mode, you can explore two algorithms for solving the puzzle:

• Backtracking: A bruteforce algorithm that solves the puzzle through trial and error.
• Deduction: A constraint propagation algorithm which applies logical rules and constraints to eliminate possibilities until the solution is found.
