About the Colorful N-Queens Puzzle

The Colorful N-Queens puzzle (created by LinkedIn as the "Queens" game) is a variation of the classical N-Queens problem.

The puzzle involves placing N colored queens on an NxN chessboard such that:
• Exactly one queen is placed in each row, column, and color region.
• No two queens are adjacent to each other (including diagonally)

In this website, you can play the puzzle and explore different algorithms related to solving and/or generating the puzzle. To start, select one of the modes above.

Play Mode



In play mode, you can click on the cells to try to solve the puzzle.

Clicking on a cell to mark it with a ♛, x, or leaving it empty. You may mark a cell with an x to indicate where no queen should be placed. If a queen is placed in an invalid position, the conflicting cells will be highlighted.

Solve Mode

In solve mode, you can explore two algorithms for solving the puzzle:

• Backtracking: A bruteforce algorithm that solves the puzzle through trial and error.
• Deduction: A constraint propagation algorithm which applies logical rules and constraints to eliminate possibilities until the solution is found.
